Behind the Look

Sadie Sink x Stranger Things Season 3 Premiere

Dear Babes,

When I first started in Fashion in New York City, the thing that amazed me most was how much time, how many people, and how much labor that went into creating each “Look.” From arranging multiple fittings with tailors + designers, to communicating with FedEx and customs to ensure that the look arrives from Milan or Paris to LA in time, to dyeing the shoe the perfect hue of pink so it matches the look to a T – it truly does take a village to make every red carpet moment come together.


Check out the process of how we brought a custom PRADA premiere look for Stranger Things' Sadie Sink to life!

We love Prada. Sadie just went to their Resort Fashion Show in NYC (check out her cute biker short look on Shop the Look!).

We thought they would be perfect for her ST3 premiere, so we reached out to them in an email.

Once Prada showed interest, we then sent them inspiration photos to show them what we envisioned for her premiere look.

I really loved the idea of her in some sort of a cape. And we both loved the color of light pink.

Prada came back with several amazing sketches! So we decided to move forth with this one. Next up is picking the fabric + color!

First fitting is always the most exciting as it’s the first time we are seeing the sketch come to life! Prada had made us two different bottom options to go w the top – biker shorts + pants! We decided to move forth with the pants.

We also picked the clutch + the shoe. We wanted the look to be a bit monochromatic, so we decided to get the clutch and shoe dyed to match the pink pants.

These later fittings are all about looking out for potential problems--before it's too late! We'll ensure the alterations went smoothly and that the dress fits perfectly.

This is also when we turn our focus to accessories. We'll pick out the perfect jewelry, and discuss hair/makeup ideas that will complement the look.

I try to make the premiere day as calm and seamless as possible!

All of the preparation pays off — we know everything from the earrings she’s wearing to the color of her manicure.

Here's the final look! It really does take a village, but it's so worth it!
